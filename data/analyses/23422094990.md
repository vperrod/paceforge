## What to improve

**1. Complete the distance target first, then add strides.** Tonight's run ended 3.5 km early with HR showing clear room to continue (max HR 131 vs. 195 max). If time was the constraint, shorten the stride component (4×100 m instead of 6) before shortening the easy base. Strides are high-value per minute — a 4-minute stride block matters more than the last 10 minutes of easy running.

**2. Slow the easy run down to the 4:50/km target.** Splits 2–4 averaged 4:22–4:24/km, which is 25–28 sec/km faster than plan and sits just above your lactate threshold pace (LT speed ~3:86 min/km from profile). For aerobic base building and volume accumulation, you want 4:50–5:00/km — conversational, Zone 2 dominant. The faster pace partially explains the Zone 3 dominance (10.6 min vs. 8.2 min in Zone 2).

**3. Address cadence — a persistent limiter.** At 154 spm, this is the fourth consecutive easy run below 158 spm (recent comparables: 153.8, 154.3, 155.6, 157.6 spm). The ideal aerobic cadence is 170–180 spm. At 154 spm with a 146 cm stride and GCT of 269 ms, you are spending more time on the ground per step than necessary. The fix: on your next easy run, use a 174 spm metronome cue for the first 10 minutes. You'll feel like you're shuffling — that's correct form. Stride length stays the same or grows naturally; cadence carries the load. This change alone will reduce GCT, lower vertical oscillation (currently 10.9 cm, target <9 cm), and improve running economy without increasing HR.
